Shwe Yin Aye (Coconut Milk Ice Dessert)

Shwe Yin Aye is a refreshing Burmese cold dessert made with coconut milk, jelly, sago pearls, pandan-flavored noodles, and bread cubes. Sweet, creamy, and served with ice, it is especially popular during hot weather.

Full Recipe

Prep Time15 minutes
Cooking Time15 minutes
Servings4

Nutrients per serving

Calories280 kcal
Protein3 g
Fat12 g
Carbs40 g
Sodium120 mg

Ingredients

  • Coconut milk – 2 cups
  • Sugar – 4 tbsp
  • Sago pearls – ½ cup
  • Agar jelly – 1 cup, cubed
  • Pandan noodles – 1 cup (optional)
  • Bread – 2 slices, cubed
  • Salt – a pinch
  • Ice cubes – as needed

Steps

  1. Cook sago pearls in boiling water until translucent, then rinse in cold water.
  2. Heat coconut milk with sugar and a pinch of salt until dissolved; cool completely.
  3. Prepare agar jelly and cut into small cubes.
  4. In serving bowls, add sago, jelly, pandan noodles, and bread cubes.
  5. Pour chilled coconut milk over the ingredients.
  6. Top with ice and serve cold.

Mont Lone Yay Baw (Burmese Glutinous Rice Balls)

Mont Lone Yay Baw are traditional Burmese sweet glutinous rice balls filled with jaggery or plum sugar. Commonly served during festive occasions and celebrations, they are chewy, mildly sweet, and topped with fresh coconut.

Full Recipe

Prep Time15 minutes
Cooking Time10 minutes
Servings4

Nutrients per serving

Calories220 kcal
Protein3 g
Fat4 g
Carbs40 g
Sodium10 mg

Ingredients

  • Glutinous rice flour – 2 cups
  • Water – about 1 cup (adjust as needed)
  • Plum sugar – 6–7 pieces, chopped
  • Fresh grated coconut – ½ cup (for topping)
  • Salt – a pinch

Steps

  1. Mix glutinous rice flour with water and salt to form a smooth dough.
  2. Take small portions of dough, flatten, and fill with plum sugar.
  3. Roll into balls and seal well.
  4. Bring a pot of water to a boil and gently drop in the rice balls.
  5. When the balls float (about 3–5 minutes), they are cooked.
  6. Transfer to cold water briefly, then drain.
  7. Sprinkle with grated coconut and serve.

Htoe Mont

Htoe Mont is a traditional Burmese sticky rice cake made with glutinous rice, sugar, butter or oil, raisins, and nuts. It has a dense and chewy texture, similar to mochi, and is commonly enjoyed during festivals and celebrations.

Full Recipe

Prep Time15 minutes
Cooking Time60 minutes
Servings8

Nutrients per serving

Calories320 kcal
Protein4 g
Fat12 g
Carbs50 g
Sodium150 mg

Ingredients

  • Glutinous rice flour – 3 cups
  • Sugar – 1 cup
  • Water – 3 cups
  • Butter or vegetable oil – 4 tbsp
  • Coconut milk – 1 cup
  • Raisins – ½ cup
  • Cashews or peanuts – ½ cup, roasted
  • Salt – a pinch
  • Fresh grated coconut – ½ cup (for topping)

Steps

  1. Fry the grated coconut until lightly browned; set aside to cool.
  2. Mix glutinous rice flour, sugar, salt, water, and oil until smooth.
  3. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly until sticky.
  4. Add butter, raisins, and nuts; stir well on higher heat.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a greased tray and press firmly.
  6. Allow to cool completely, then cut into squares.

Coconut Jelly

Coconut Jelly is a light and refreshing Burmese dessert made with agar-agar, coconut milk, and sugar. It has a bouncy texture with a delicate coconut flavor and is best served chilled.

Full Recipe

Prep Time5 minutes
Cooking Time10 minutes (+ cooling)
Servings6

Nutrients per serving

Calories120 kcal
Protein1 g
Fat6 g
Carbs14 g
Sodium30 mg

Ingredients

  • Coconut milk – 1½ cups
  • Water – 1½ cups
  • Agar-agar powder – 1 tbsp
  • Sugar – 4 tbsp
  • Salt – a pinch

Steps

  1. In a pot, mix water with agar-agar powder and heat until dissolved.
  2. Add sugar and stir until fully melted.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and a pinch of salt; simmer gently.
  4. Remove from heat and pour into molds or a tray.
  5. Cool at room temperature, then refrigerate until firm.
  6. Cut into cubes and serve chilled.
